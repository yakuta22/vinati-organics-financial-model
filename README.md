## Project Overview

A complete bottom-up financial model built from scratch on Vinati Organics Limited — India's largest producer of IBB (Ibuprofen Intermediate) and ATBS (used in water treatment and specialty polymers). The model covers 6 years of historical data (FY2020–FY2025) and 3 years of projections (FY2026E–FY2028E), with a full DCF valuation.

**Every number in this model was manually extracted and verified from BSE India annual reports. No data was copied from financial aggregators without cross-verification.**

## Investment Thesis Summary

**Why Vinati?**
- Global market leader in IBB and ATBS — genuine pricing power, not a commodity producer
- Zero effective debt on a ₹2,800+ Crore equity base — financial fortress
- FY2024 revenue decline was cyclical (global destocking), not structural — confirmed by FY2025 actual recovery of 18.3%

**Bull Case Drivers**
- China Plus One: India faces 26% US tariffs vs China's 54% — India's relative advantage is widening
- ATBS capacity expansion completed in FY2025 — volume growth driver for FY2026 onwards
- India specialty chemicals CAGR of 12.2% supported by Viksit Bharat policy push

**Key Risks**
- Raw material cost volatility (crude oil linked)
- Customer concentration (~40 global clients)
- Chinese chemical dumping creating short-term pricing pressure

## Assumptions Methodology

All projection assumptions were derived using a 4-step framework:

1. **Historical ratios** — Calculated each cost line as % of revenue for 5 years to identify trends
2. **MD&A analysis** — Read management commentary to understand drivers behind ratio movements
3. **Peer benchmarking** — Referenced sector CAGR data and industry reports
4. **FY2025 actual verification** — Updated all FY2025E assumptions with actual annual report data
